What is an On-Grid Solar System?

An on-grid solar system, also known as a grid-tied system, is connected directly to your local utility grid. This is the most popular choice for Indian households because it allows you to use the grid as a virtual battery—exporting excess power during the day and drawing power at night.

PM Surya Ghar Support

On-grid systems are eligible for central government subsidies under the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana, making them highly affordable.

How Does It Work?

The system works in a seamless cycle with your utility provider:

  • Generation: Solar panels capture sunlight and convert it into DC electricity.
  • Conversion: The On-Grid Inverter converts DC power into AC power for your home appliances.
  • Usage: Your home uses the solar energy first. Any extra power is sent to the grid.
  • Grid Balance: At night or on rainy days, you automatically import power from the grid.

Modern inverters ensure that the solar power produced matches the grid's voltage and frequency perfectly.

Major Components

DCR Solar Modules

High-efficiency panels that meet government requirements for subsidies.

On-Grid Inverter

The "heart" of the system that manages power flow and synchronizes with the grid.

Bi-directional Meter

A special meter that tracks both the electricity you consume and the electricity you export.

Safety Gear

Includes AC/DC boxes, chemical earthing, and lightning arrestors to protect your home.

Understanding Net Metering

Net metering is the financial mechanism that makes On-Grid systems so profitable. It replaces your standard meter with a Bi-directional meter.

How the Billing Works:

Total Units Consumed - Total Units Exported = Your Net Bill

If you produce more than you consume in a month, the excess "units" are carried forward to the next month's bill, further reducing your future costs.

Why Go On-Grid?

Fastest ROI

The system pays for itself in 3-5 years, followed by 20+ years of free electricity.

Low Maintenance

No batteries are required, which simplifies the system and reduces long-term costs.

On-grid systems also feature "Anti-Islanding" protection, which automatically shuts the system down during grid failures to keep repair workers safe.
